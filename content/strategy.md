# Investment Strategy & Process

Sapiens Alpha's India Fund of Funds (FoF) uses a systematic, rules-based quantitative framework to build an equally-weighted portfolio of four carefully selected Indian mutual funds, benchmarked against the S&P BSE 500 Index.

## Philosophy — Four Pillars

Our process rests on four core principles: maximising risk-adjusted returns by prioritising Sharpe and Sortino ratios over raw performance; minimising inter-fund correlation to avoid thematic or style overlaps across market cycles; avoiding overfitting through rigorous five-year out-of-sample forward simulations that validate robustness across regimes; and restricting the investable universe to institutional-quality managers with 10+ years of track records and at least ₹3,000 Cr in AUM.

## Screening & Selection

Starting from India's full mutual fund universe, Level 1 screening narrows the field to approximately 189 direct-plan equity and flexi-cap schemes meeting size and longevity thresholds. Level 2 applies our quantitative scoring model — using Sharpe and Sortino ratios with a five-year lookback — to identify four uncorrelated funds with correlation mostly below 0.9: two growth-oriented and two conservative. This Balanced Architecture maximises overall portfolio alpha while constraining volatility and drawdown. An annual rebalancing cost of 0.5% p.a. is incorporated in all performance simulations to account for operational leakage.

## Simulation Results (2020–25 Out-of-Sample)

The Balanced Architecture delivered a **2.32% annualised alpha** over the BSE 500 with **4.05% lower volatility** and a **6.48% lower maximum drawdown** versus the benchmark — demonstrating that active quantitative fund selection can compound returns while protecting against sharp drawdowns.
