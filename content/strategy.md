Sapiens Alpha's India Fund of Funds employs a systematic, rules-based quantitative process to construct an equally-weighted portfolio of four Indian mutual funds benchmarked against the S&P BSE 500. Starting from a screened universe of approximately 189 direct-plan equity and flexi-cap schemes — filtered to managers with 10+ year track records and ₹3,000 Cr or more in AUM — the model applies a five-year lookback scoring framework that prioritises Sharpe and Sortino ratios to identify four uncorrelated funds, each with inter-fund correlations consistently below 0.9. The portfolio pairs two growth-oriented and two conservative funds, collectively maximising risk-adjusted alpha while constraining overall volatility and drawdown. Robustness is validated through rigorous out-of-sample forward simulations, and a 0.5% per annum operational cost is incorporated in all performance projections. This Balanced Architecture approach has historically delivered a 2.32% annualised alpha over the BSE 500, with 4.05% lower volatility and a 6.48% shallower maximum drawdown, rebalanced annually across the 2020–25 simulation period.
